Research

I develop chemical tools—spanning synthetic organic chemistry and protein engineering—and apply them in living systems to enable biological discovery and novel therapeutic strategies.

Areas

1) Protein lipidation & S-palmitoylation

Tool compounds and mechanistic studies to probe writers/erasers and functional consequences of S-palmitoylation on stability, localization, and signaling.

Example outputs: selective enzyme inhibitors, substrate-tracking assays, chemical probes.

2) Proximity labeling for RNA / protein interactions

Non-radical proximity labeling approaches to capture local biomolecular neighborhoods with improved specificity and compatibility in cells.

Example outputs: proximity-enabled labeling chemistries, targeted reporters, analysis pipelines.

3) Chemical-induced proximity for therapy

Proximity-based therapeutic modalities that expand beyond inhibition and degradation, including bivalent chemical inducers of proximity and DNA damage–linked proximity strategies.

Example outputs: degrader-like systems, induced proximity therapeutics, translational concepts.

Keywords: chemical biology, proximity labeling, induced proximity, degraders, DNA damage signaling, palmitoylation.
